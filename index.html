Suvansh Sanjeev

I am a first-year PhD student in the Robotics Institute at Carnegie Mellon University, where I am advised by Zico Kolter and Zac Manchester. I am interested in artificial intelligence, machine learning, and optimization. I graduated from UC Berkeley, where I worked with Professors Sergey Levine and Claire Tomlin in the Berkeley Artificial Intelligence Research (BAIR) Lab on deep RL and safe learning.

Interests

I am currently working on Brilliantly, providing custom AI solutions for clients. Most recently, we launched SightBot, a demo chatbot that converses with users on biomedical research topics and cites its sources from PubMed.

My past research worked towards bringing reinforcement learning to the real world. Most recently, I worked on gray-box methods for control of quadrotors. I have also worked on developing more natural means of task specification for deep RL to avoid the burden of manually engineered reward functions, as well as on developing data-efficient learning techniques that allow for safety guarantees throughout the learning process.
